Since 1927

A Gift to the City

Candler Park Golf Course was donated to the City of Atlanta by Coca-Cola founder Asa Candler. It opened in 1927 specifically so his daughter could play at a time when women were excluded from nearby private clubs.

Today, it remains one of the most casual and welcoming places to play golf in Atlanta—a true "muni" in the best sense of the word.

A Refreshing Vision

We don't want to turn Candler Park into a country club. We want to make it the best version of what it already is.

Immediately Accessible

Affordable rates, walkable, and welcoming for first timers, families, and casual play.

Fun at Every Level

Architecturally thoughtful so beginners feel included and better players stay engaged.

Community Integrated

Neighborhood kids in the shop. Birthday parties on the patio. Coffee worth walking over for.

Welcoming to Women

Honoring the legacy of Asa Candler’s daughter with programming that gives women ownership.

Short Range Plan

What We're Doing Now

In the short term, our focus is on making the day-to-day better.

1. Patio Project

Partnering with the city on a ~$100k renovation to turn the back of the clubhouse into a place people actually want to sit, linger, and host events.

2. Coffee & Pop Ups

Bringing in local partners to run coffee mornings and casual food offerings that match the neighborhood vibe.

3. Annual Classic

Growing the Candler Park Golf Classic as a signature neighborhood fundraiser.

The Long Range View

Building a sustainable future.

NOW
